WORK

Graduate Teaching Assistant
Montana State Univeristy

I taught one semester classes on introductory and intermediate statistics classes during Fall 2013, Spring 2014, and Spring 2014. In these courses, I prepared lectures, worksheets, online assignments, and online in-class activities three times a week for 40 students. These courses provided an introduction to hypothesis tests, confidence intervals, the Central Limit Theorem, probability theory, chi-squared testing, and linear regression. Over 80% of the students who enrolled in my section passed which is above average for introductory statistics courses at Montana State University.

AUGUST 2013 - PRESENT

Baseball Operations Intern
Philadelphia Phillies

I was employed from May through December 2014 as an intern in the Baseball Operations department of the Philadelphia Phillies. My primary task was to build a proprietary tool which could be used to identify comparable players to a player of interest. This tool was then extended to allow it to simulate how the performance of the player of interest would change as he ages. I also analyzed defensive metrics, aging curves, and arbitration for the Phillies. In addition to these analyses, I aided with player tracking, arbitration meetings, and injury analytics.

MAY 2014 - DECEMBER 2014

Summer Intern
Jet Propulsion Laboratory

I was hired by the business department at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory to help forecast the cost of space missions. I used a mixed effects model with a cubic polynomial to analyze the expenditures of 12 missions that launched between 2001 and 2012. I then used this model to project the cost of a satellite that was currently being built. I used both R and SQL to complete this project. The results of this research were favorably received by Division Managers and other summer interns.
